# Meta

While I don't think there should be a single definite way to interpret the WisdomDump. I will expand some of the ideas here to provide more context.

## coffee

The first degree to this is that you can burn yourself pretty bad if you hold the cup in the air with one hand and pour with the other hand. This was one of the first thing that I was taught while working at McDonald's a long time ago. From what I can notice though, it's something that people don't apply naturally. It's really common to not set the cup down first. It's a case where you can easily avoid clumsiness with a simple change.

The deeper idea is that this can be applied to many other concepts. How many times do we make ours lives more clumsy from not reducing the variables that we control? What other things are you "holding into the air"? The step of "placing something down" feels like a waste of time when trying to go fast but it actually simplifies the task and reduces risk.

## sunglasses

I don't think I had any deeper meaning with that one. It's just a generally good idea to protect your eyes.
